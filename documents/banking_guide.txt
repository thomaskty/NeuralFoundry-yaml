# Banking Fundamentals Guide

## What is a Bank Account?

A bank account is a financial account that allows individuals and businesses to deposit, manage, and withdraw money. Banks provide various types of accounts to serve different financial needs of their customers.

## Types of Bank Accounts

### 1. Savings Account
- **Purpose**: For regular individuals to save money and earn interest
- **Features**: 
  - Moderate interest rates (4-6% annually)
  - Limited number of transactions
  - Overdraft facility available
  - Suitable for daily banking needs
- **Minimum Balance**: Usually INR 1,000 to 5,000

### 2. Current Account
- **Purpose**: For businesses and high-value transactions
- **Features**:
  - No interest earned
  - Unlimited transactions
  - Overdraft facility of up to 50% of approved limit
  - Suitable for frequent business transactions
- **Minimum Balance**: Usually INR 10,000 to 25,000

### 3. Money Market Account
- **Purpose**: Combines features of savings and checking accounts
- **Features**:
  - Higher interest rates
  - Limited check writing
  - Minimum balance requirement
  - Access to ATM and debit card

### 4. Fixed Deposit Account
- **Purpose**: For secure investments with guaranteed returns
- **Features**:
  - Fixed tenure (3 months to 5 years)
  - Higher interest rates (5-8% depending on tenure)
  - Penalty for premature withdrawal
  - Principal amount is secure

## Online Banking

Modern banks provide online banking facilities that allow customers to:
- Check account balance
- Transfer funds between accounts
- Pay bills online
- Download statements
- Apply for loans
- Set up recurring payments
- Monitor transactions 24/7

## Charges Associated with Bank Accounts

### Common Charges:
- Account opening charges: Usually free
- Annual maintenance charges: INR 0 - 2,000
- ATM withdrawal charges: Free for own bank, INR 20-50 for others
- SMS notification charges: INR 30-50 per month
- Cheque book charges: INR 5-20 per cheque
- Wire transfer charges: INR 25-100 depending on amount

## Documents Required to Open a Bank Account

1. **Identity Proof**: Passport, Aadhaar, Voter ID, DL, PAN
2. **Address Proof**: Utility bill, Rental agreement, Passport
3. **Photograph**: Recent passport-sized photograph (2-3 copies)
4. **Pan Card**: Mandatory for transactions above a certain limit
5. **Income Documents**: Salary slip, ITR, business registration

## Safety and Security Tips

- Keep your PIN and password confidential
- Use strong passwords with mix of letters, numbers, symbols
- Never share OTP with anyone
- Regularly check statement for unauthorized transactions
- Use two-factor authentication when available
- Update contact information with bank
- Report lost cards immediately

## Overdraft Facility

An overdraft facility allows customers to withdraw more than their account balance up to a certain limit. 

- **Eligibility**: Good credit history and regular salary deposits
- **Limit**: Usually 1-3 months salary
- **Interest Rate**: Higher than regular interest (8-12% annually)
- **Usage Fee**: May apply for utilizing overdraft facility

## Internet Banking Security

Banks implement several security measures:
- SSL encryption for all transactions
- Multi-factor authentication
- Biometric login options
- Transaction alerts via SMS/email
- Regular fraud monitoring
- Customer grievance redressal system

## Common Banking Services

1. **Remittance Services**: Send money domestically or internationally
2. **Investment Products**: Mutual funds, stocks, bonds
3. **Loan Products**: Personal, home, auto, education loans
4. **Insurance Products**: Life, health, property insurance
5. **Credit Cards**: Various cards with different rewards programs
6. **Debit Cards**: For easy access to account balance
7. **Safe Deposit Lockers**: For storing valuables and documents
